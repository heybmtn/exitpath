Exit Path

The questionnaire

Start with what you already know.

No business idea needed. Just honest, specific answers about what you do, what people come to you for, and what feels easy. The more concrete you are, the sharper your result.

01

You

So I can get back to you to set up our session.
02

What you actually do

The things people rely on you to handle, fix, figure out, organise, or smooth over — at work, at home, anywhere.
The thing you almost dismiss because it’s obvious to you — that’s usually the gold.
03

The receipts

What was it? What did you actually do, step by step? What changed because of you?
The actual phrases they use. “I can’t get my head round…”, “Can you just…”, “How do you always…” The way someone names a problem is the way they’ll search for the answer.
Unprompted demand is the strongest signal there is. Who, and what did they say? If it’s never happened, say so plainly.
Even once, even small. Describe it — and roughly how much, if you remember.
More demand than you could meet is a strong sign there’s a market. What was it, and how often does it happen?
04

Your track record

Jobs, running a home, raising kids, a hobby you went deep on, volunteering, study — all of it counts. We’re after the pattern, not a list.
In or outside paid work — including anything tangible, like a system, a template, a spreadsheet, a group, or a piece of writing.
05

Energy

06

How you’re wired

A few honest words. Don’t polish it — the real ones are the useful ones.
Think about: alone or around people, structured or flexible, steady routine or constant variety, behind the scenes or front and centre, slow and careful or fast and rough.
The things you’d happily read about, tinker with, or talk about for hours.
Be honest — this rules options out, which is just as useful as ruling them in.
Your real dealbreakers. If there are none, say so.
07

The exit you want

No problem if not — that’s what this is for.
Side projects, businesses, ideas that fizzled — where did it stall, and why? No judgement. Knowing what’s tripped you up before is one of the most useful things you can tell me.
Any group you could contact without starting from scratch — a network, an old client list, a community or group you’re part of, colleagues, an audience, even a busy WhatsApp thread. Note if any of them already have the problem you’d be solving. If there’s honestly no one, say so.
A warm handful who’ll actually reply beats a big list who won’t. Be realistic.
The actual channel — so the first move is something you could do, not a plan you’d need to build first.
Money you’d be comfortable risking up front — even nothing is a useful answer — alongside the time you give below.
The monthly take-home — after tax — that would simply replace what you bring in now, or let you stop the thing you most want to stop. The point where you could breathe, even if it’s not yet “free”. You’ll likely hit this long before your freedom number.
The monthly income — in your pocket, after tax — that would let you feel genuinely free. A rough figure is fine.
Hard constraints, skills you want to learn or avoid, anything I haven’t asked.
08

Your runway

Savings, a partner’s income, your current job, a pension. Roughly how many months could you keep going if this brought in nothing at first? Be honest — it changes the route completely.
Different from your ideal timeline above. This is about pressure, not ambition.

Your answers come straight to me. I’ll read them, map the leverage hiding in them, and we’ll go through it together.
